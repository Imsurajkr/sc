## Role of Amicus Curiae in Indian Courts

The amicus curiae plays a particularly important role in Indian constitutional courts, especially in cases involving constitutional interpretation, PIL matters, and situations where vulnerable populations are affected. In the Sabarimala Review case, several senior advocates were appointed as amici to assist the nine-judge bench. In the same-sex marriage case, the court appointed amici to present arguments not covered by the parties.

## Distinction from Intervenors

An amicus curiae differs from an intervenor, who is a person or organisation that has a sufficient interest in the proceedings to be joined as a party. An amicus presents arguments to assist the court but does not advocate for any particular outcome in the same way as a party. However, in practice, the distinction can sometimes blur when amici are appointed to represent the interests of a particular constituency.

## Court-Appointed vs. Applied-For

In India, the court typically appoints amici in complex matters of public importance on its own initiative. However, individuals and organisations can also apply to be heard as amicus curiae, and the court exercises discretion in accepting such applications. The appointment of a well-regarded senior advocate as amicus can significantly shape the direction of judicial discourse in a case.
