## Scope of Certiorari in India

The scope of certiorari under Article 226 of the Indian Constitution is wider than its counterpart in English law. Indian courts have expanded the writ to include errors of law on the face of the record, violations of natural justice, and decisions made without considering relevant material. The distinction between a mere error of law and an error going to jurisdiction has been progressively narrowed.

## Certiorari and Natural Justice

One of the most significant grounds for certiorari in India is the violation of natural justice — specifically the principles of audi alteram partem (hear the other side) and nemo judex in causa sua (no one should be a judge in their own cause). An order passed without giving the affected party an opportunity to be heard is generally amenable to being quashed by certiorari.
