## Article 136 of the Constitution

Article 136 is among the most broadly worded provisions in the Indian Constitution. It gives the Supreme Court the power to grant special leave to appeal from any judgment, decree, determination, sentence, or order in any cause or matter passed or made by any court or tribunal in the territory of India. The only explicit exclusion is courts or tribunals constituted by or under any law relating to the Armed Forces.

## Discretionary Nature

The grant of special leave is entirely at the discretion of the Supreme Court. The court will not ordinarily interfere merely because it might have decided the matter differently. Leave is typically granted when:
- There is a substantial question of law of general importance.
- The case involves a question upon which the Supreme Court's pronouncement is needed.
- There has been a manifest miscarriage of justice.
- The lower court's approach is perverse or ignores binding precedent.

## The SLP as the Gateway

The SLP has become the primary gateway to the Supreme Court, with thousands filed every year. The court typically entertains around 60,000-70,000 SLPs annually and admits only a fraction for full hearing. The high volume of SLPs is itself a subject of debate — it contributes to the court's enormous docket burden and has led to calls for a reform of the Supreme Court into a constitutional court that hears only matters of constitutional importance.
