---
term: "Locus Standi"
definition: "The legal capacity or right of a person or organisation to bring an action in a court of law. A party must demonstrate sufficient connection to and harm from a law or action before they have the standing to challenge it. Also known as 'standing to sue'."
etymology: "From Latin meaning 'place of standing' — referring to the petitioner's recognised position before the court."
example: "A trade union files a public interest litigation before the Supreme Court on behalf of unorganised workers, arguing that the union has locus standi to represent their interests even though no individual worker has filed a petition."

## Traditional Locus Standi in India

Under the traditional common law rule, only a person who has suffered a personal injury or infringement of their own legal rights could file a petition in court. This requirement was strictly enforced by Indian courts in the decades following independence, limiting access to justice to those who could afford legal representation and who had clearly established personal interests.

## The PIL Revolution and Expanded Standing

The concept of locus standi was fundamentally transformed in India through the public interest litigation (PIL) jurisprudence developed by Justices P.N. Bhagwati and V.R. Krishna Iyer in the late 1970s and 1980s. The court held that any public-spirited individual could file a petition on behalf of those who were unable to access justice themselves — prisoners, bonded labourers, slum dwellers, and others on the margins of society. This expanded standing has been described as one of the most significant procedural innovations in Indian legal history.

## Misuse and Judicial Response

The liberalisation of locus standi has also led to misuse, with PILs being filed for oblique motives including harassment of opponents, seeking publicity, or delaying legitimate activities. The Supreme Court has in recent years imposed costs on frivolous PILs and emphasised the need for petitioners to demonstrate genuine public interest rather than personal or political agendas.
