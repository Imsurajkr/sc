term: "Mandamus"

## Elements of Mandamus

For a writ of mandamus to lie, three elements must be established:
1. The petitioner has a legal right to the performance of the act.
2. The respondent has a corresponding legal duty to perform that act.
3. The respondent has failed or refused to perform that duty.

Mandamus lies only for ministerial duties — those that are mandatory and leave no discretion to the authority. It does not lie to compel the exercise of discretion in a particular way, though it can compel an authority to exercise its discretion.

## Mandamus Against Private Bodies

Traditionally, mandamus lay only against public authorities. However, Indian courts have progressively extended it to cover bodies that perform public functions, even if they are private entities. Universities, regulatory bodies, and entities exercising monopoly power over essential services have been brought within the ambit of mandamus through a purposive interpretation.
