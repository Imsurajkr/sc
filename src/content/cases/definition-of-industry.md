## Background

The definition of "industry" under the Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 has been a matter of significant legal debate since the landmark Bangalore Water Supply and Sewerage Board v. A. Rajappa (1978) judgment. In that case, a seven-judge Constitution Bench had laid down an expansive definition that brought within the Act's purview a wide range of activities.

## Legal Issues

The primary question before the current Constitution Bench is whether the expansive definition laid down in Bangalore Water Supply continues to hold good, or whether it needs to be revisited in light of changed economic and social conditions. The court must also determine:

1. Whether sovereign and regal functions of the state can be brought within the definition of industry.
2. Whether institutions primarily engaged in charitable or religious activities qualify as industries.
3. How the 2010 amendment to the Industrial Disputes Act affects the scope of the definition.

## Current Status

The Constitution Bench has heard several rounds of arguments from both management and union sides. The matter has been referred to a larger bench due to conflicting precedents and is scheduled for further hearing in May 2026.
