## Background

In May 2024, following the NEET-UG examination, serious allegations emerged about paper leaks in multiple states. Students and organisations filed petitions in the Supreme Court seeking cancellation of the entire examination and a fresh re-test. The National Testing Agency (NTA), which conducts the exam, faced severe criticism for its handling of the situation.

## Court Proceedings

The Supreme Court heard the matter on an urgent basis. Petitioners argued that the scale of the paper leak was so large that the integrity of the entire examination was compromised, making a re-test essential. The government and NTA argued that the leak was localised to specific centres.

## The Judgment

The three-judge bench, in a detailed judgment, held:

- The petitioners had not established that the paper leak was so widespread as to have benefited candidates across the country.
- "Systemic breach" of examination integrity would require compelling evidence going beyond specific instances.
- The court cannot order a re-test — affecting 23 lakh students — based on speculation.
- However, results at specific centres where irregularities were proven were to be cancelled.

## Institutional Reforms

The court endorsed the formation of a high-level committee under former ISRO chairman K. Radhakrishnan to review the NTA's examination processes. The committee's recommendations led to significant reforms in how competitive examinations are conducted in India.

## Significance

The case highlighted the vulnerability of India's high-stakes examination system to corruption and the devastating consequences for millions of students. The judgment balanced individual rights against the practical impossibility of conducting a re-test while setting important standards for examination integrity.
